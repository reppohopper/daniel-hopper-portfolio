About

I’m a software engineer and game developer with a background in building practical tools and playable prototypes that prioritize clarity and iteration. I started tinkering with game development in high school and pursued formal CS coursework and independent projects afterward, learning how to move from idea to a working, testable product. Over time I found I enjoy both the systems-level work—designing reliable APIs and tooling—and the craft of interaction design in games that feel responsive and intentional.

My technical interests include performant gameplay systems in Unity (C#), small cross-platform tools written in Go, and clean, accessible web front-ends for demos and documentation. I care about maintainable code, automated workflows, and readable documentation so future collaborators can pick up work quickly. Professionally, I value clear communication, shipping incremental improvements, and using metrics and feedback to guide decisions.

Outside of engineering I enjoy hiking, weight training, and sketching level ideas—activities that help me recharge and return to problems with fresh perspective. I’m actively looking to contribute to teams building tooling or interactive experiences where pragmatic engineering and user-centered design meet.
